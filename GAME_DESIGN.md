# Veilbreak Bureau
### A Procedural Detective Roguelike

---

## 1. Vision Statement

> *"Every case is real. Every culprit is guilty. The clues fit — they always fit — but only one of you is going to put them in the right order."*

**Veilbreak Bureau** is a single-player procedural detective roguelike. Each run, the game generates a fully self-consistent occult crime in an alt-history 1920s city — with suspects, motives, alibis, locations, weapons, and timelines that genuinely logically resolve to one culprit. You investigate. You accuse. You solve, you fail, or worse: you solve the wrong case. Between runs you climb the bureau ranks, unlock investigative tools, and confront the larger conspiracy that no single case can crack.

Imagine *Return of the Obra Dinn*'s deductive elegance, *Hades*' run-based meta-progression, *Disco Elysium*'s prose, and *Dwarf Fortress*'s emergent storytelling fused into one tightly-scoped 30-minute case loop.

## 3. Core Mechanics

### 3.1 The Case Loop (25–45 min per case)

1. **Briefing** – At the Veilbreak Bureau, your Inspector hands you a case file. Victim, location, time of death, initial impressions.
2. **The Scene** – Examine the crime scene. 4–8 examinable objects. Each yields a *clue card* with one to three facts.
3. **The Roster** – 4–7 procedurally generated suspects with relationships, occupations, secrets, alibis.
4. **Investigation** – Travel between 5–8 city locations. Interview suspects. Examine secondary scenes. Cross-reference clues at your Pinboard.
5. **The Pinboard** – Your detective workspace. Drag clues onto suspects, weapons, locations to build hypotheses. Connections that don't fit *visibly fail*.
6. **The Accusation** – Once confident, accuse a suspect with a complete chain: *Suspect + Weapon + Motive + Opportunity*. Get any wrong → case closed unfavorably.
7. **The Verdict** – Procedural sentencing scene plays out. The bureau ranks you. You take what you learned — about the world, your tools, and yourself — into the next case.

### 3.2 Pillar Mechanics

#### A) The Mystery Generator (Procedural Core)
The heart of the game. Every case is generated by a **3-pass system**:

1. **World Pass**: Sample suspects from a 200+ NPC pool. Each NPC has occupation, social graph, secrets, possible motives.
2. **Crime Pass**: Choose victim. Among suspects with motive-against-victim, choose culprit via weighted social-graph logic. Generate weapon (occupation-appropriate) and timeline.
3. **Evidence Pass**: Distribute clue cards across scenes. Guarantee at least one *uniquely-implicating* clue per case-element (suspect, weapon, motive, opportunity). Generate plausible red herrings tied to non-culprit suspects.

**Critical guarantee**: every case has *exactly one logically consistent solution* and the player can always reach it through legitimate deduction. No guessing required.

**Inspired by**: Wave Function Collapse, *Bad News* (Tracery-based simulation), Versu (Inkle).

#### B) The Pinboard (Deduction Core)
Your tactile detective workspace. Diegetic Pinboard with:
- **Suspect cards** (4–7 per case)
- **Clue cards** (15–30 per case)
- **String connections** drawn by player linking clue → suspect/weapon/motive

When you pin a clue against a suspect:
- **Green pulse** if the connection logically holds
- **Red flicker** if it directly contradicts another connection
- **Yellow** if ambiguous (insufficient supporting evidence)

This is the diegetic equivalent of Obra Dinn's "three correct fates locks in" — but live, dynamic, and visual.

#### C) The Bureau (Meta-Progression Core)
Between cases, your detective lives at the Bureau. Persistent progression:
- **Rank**: Constable → Sergeant → Inspector → Chief Inspector → Veilbreak Specialist
- **Tools**: Unlock magnifying glass, séance kit, ballistics microscope, occult sigil chart, time-shadow recorder
- **Skills**: 5 deduction trees (Logic, Empathy, Occult, Forensics, Streetcraft) — each affects which clues are *visible* to you
- **The Bureau Map**: 12 wings unlocked over campaign. Each wing = a chapter of meta-narrative.
- **The Conspiracy**: A persistent investigation board that *every* case contributes a thread to. The grand antagonist is revealed across 30–50 cases.

#### D) The Veilbreak (Occult Twist)
The world is alt-1920s with a fading occult layer. Some cases involve genuine supernatural elements that *only become visible* once Occult skill is invested. A "drowning" might be a séance gone wrong. A "robbery" might be a ritual theft.
- Mundane cases (60%) — pure detective work
- Veil-touched cases (30%) — occult tools required
- True Veilbreak cases (10%) — terrifying, high-stakes, story-critical

This keeps procedural variety high and tonal range wide (cozy → noir → cosmic horror).

#### E) Failure as Story (Roguelike Core)
A wrong accusation doesn't end the game. It ends the *case*:
- **Wrong suspect convicted**: actual culprit walks free. You get a Bureau penalty (lose ranks).
- **No accusation by deadline**: case archived as cold. Periodically reappears with new clues.
- **Right suspect, wrong charge**: lighter penalty.
- **Death**: never. Detectives retire, get reassigned, or vanish into the Veil.

Wrong calls have *narrative consequences* — the freed culprit might appear as a witness in your next case, taunting you. Cold cases haunt your Bureau wall.

## 4. Unique Selling Points

1. **First truly procedural narrative-prose detective game** — the holy grail of mystery design.
2. **Every case is fair** — no unsolvable cases, no guessing. The genre's contract is sacred.
3. **Failure has consequences, not save-scumming** — wrong accusations persist across the campaign.
4. **The conspiracy meta-narrative** — links 30–50 cases into one campaign, tying procedural to authored.
5. **The occult layer** — adds Lovecraftian tonal range without abandoning the noir core.
6. **Streamable to its bones** — viewers can play along, theorize in chat, and watch the dramatic accusation moment.

## 9. Production Pillars (Dev Constraints)

| Pillar | Rule |
|---|---|
| **The contract is sacred** | Every case must be solvable by deduction alone. We will fail-test 10,000+ generated cases before release. |
| **Procedural ≠ generic** | Every NPC, location, and motive must read as authored. Tracery-grade prose only. |
| **The Pinboard is the game** | All deduction lives on the board. No hidden state. Player always knows what they know. |
| **Streamer-shaped** | Every accusation is a dramatic moment. UI supports overlays, alt-cams, ASMR-grade SFX. |
| **Roguelike with a heart** | The meta-narrative gives wins and losses meaning. No purely mechanical runs. |

## 10. Risk Analysis & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Procedural cases feel mechanical | **High** | Hire 3+ narrative designers; build template-rich prose system; extensive playtesting |
| Players figure out the meta-system | Medium | Multiple generator variants per case-archetype; randomize tells |
| Unfair cases slip through | **Critical** | Automated solver must verify every case before delivery. No exceptions. |
| Pinboard UX is unwieldy | Medium | Iterate from day one; gather playtester data on connection-time-to-solve |
| Roguelike audience expects combat | Low | Position cleanly as "deductive roguelike" — niche but loyal |
