## Full Game Description

**Veilbreak Bureau** is a single-player procedural detective roguelike set in an alt-history 1920s city where a fading occult layer leaks into ordinary crimes. You play a detective rising through the ranks of the Veilbreak Bureau, an investigative agency tasked with mundane and supernatural cases alike.

Each case is **procedurally generated** — suspects, motives, weapons, alibis, locations, and timelines are all freshly built for every playthrough. But unlike most procedural games, **every case has exactly one logically consistent solution that can always be reached through legitimate deduction**. The Mystery Generator uses a 3-pass system (World → Crime → Evidence) and an automated solver verifies every case before serving it to the player.

### You Investigate By:

1. **Reading the briefing** — victim, location, time of death, initial impressions
2. **Examining scenes** — 4–8 procedurally-generated locations produce clue cards
3. **Interviewing suspects** — 4–7 suspects with relationships, occupations, secrets, alibis
4. **Pinning your theory** on a diegetic cork pinboard — drag clues onto suspects, watch connections light up green/red
5. **Making a final accusation** — **Suspect + Weapon + Motive + Opportunity**

### Failure Is Story, Not Game-Over

A wrong accusation doesn't end the game — it ends the case. The actual culprit walks free, your bureau rank drops, and **that freed culprit may appear as a witness in a future case, taunting you**. Cold cases periodically return with new clues. Failures persist across your campaign with narrative weight.

### Between Cases — The Bureau

Your detective lives at the bureau. Persistent meta-progression includes:

- **Rank**: Constable → Sergeant → Inspector → Chief Inspector → Veilbreak Specialist
- **Tools**: Magnifying glass, séance kit, ballistics microscope, occult sigil chart, time-shadow recorder
- **Skills**: 5 deduction trees (Logic, Empathy, Occult, Forensics, Streetcraft)
- **The Conspiracy Board** — a meta-narrative woven across 30–50 cases, revealing the bureau's true antagonist

### The Occult Layer

The world is alt-1920s with a fading occult layer:
- **Mundane cases (60%)** — pure detective work
- **Veil-touched cases (30%)** — occult tools required to see clues only visible through ritual
- **True Veilbreak cases (10%)** — story-critical, terrifying, high-stakes

This keeps tonal range wide (cozy noir → Lovecraftian horror) without breaking the core loop.
